Confidence Interval

Understanding CI

  • 95% is NOT the probablity of anything
  • μ\muμ = true popoluation mean is an unknown constant.
  • Every unique sample generates its own corresponding CI.
  • For any interval (a, b), either μ\muμ is in the interval or it is not.
  • We never know for certain whether μ\muμ is in the CI generated by a particular data set.
  • Our confidence is in the procedure for constructing the CI.

Confidence in CI Procedure

  • Choose α\alphaα
    1. Random sample of size n from population of interest
    2. Collect Data
    3. Construct (1−α)%(1-\alpha)\%(1−α)% CI corresponding to this data set.
  • Repeat steps 1-3 many, many times -> approximately (1−α)%(1-\alpha)\%(1−α)% CI contains μ\muμ, but we don't know which ones

CI connection to HT

  • CI are "reasonable values" for the population parameter
  • A two-sided HT will fail to reject H0H_0H0​ if μ0\mu_0μ0​ is reasonable for that data set.
  • If μ0\mu_0μ0​ is in the corresponding (1−α)%(1-\alpha)\%(1−α)% CI, then the HT will fail to reject H0H_0H0​.
