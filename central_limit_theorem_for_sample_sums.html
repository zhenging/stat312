Central Limit Theorem For Sample Sums

Suppose XXX is a random variable with a distribution that may be known or unknow (it can be any distribution). Using a subscript that matches the random variable, suppose:
a. $\mu_X = $ the mean of XXX
b. $\sigma_X = $ the standard deviation of XXX.

If you draw random sample of sizes nnn, then as nnn increase, the random variable ΣX\Sigma {X}ΣX consisting of sums tends to be normally distributed and
